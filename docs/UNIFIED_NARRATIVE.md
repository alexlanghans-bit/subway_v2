## The Revolutionary Idea

**Devapp1 proposes something never done in the restaurant industry:**

> Open-source our performance metrics to customers.

Every store's Success Rate, Order Accuracy, Freshness scores, Speed of Service â€” visible to anyone. At 20,000+ locations. In real-time.

**This is radical transparency at unprecedented scale.**

No restaurant chain has ever done this. The industry hides behind mystery shoppers, internal audits, and sanitized marketing. Subway would be saying: *"Here's how we're actually doing. Judge us."*

## Why Subway-v2 Must Exist

You cannot promise transparency without the spine to survive it.

**The transparency play exposes everything:**
- Underperforming stores become visible to customers
- Bad operators can't hide behind brand marketing
- Inconsistency across the network is laid bare
- Every failure is public

**Subway-v2 provides the strategic backbone:**

| Transparency Risk | Strategic Solution |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Bad stores exposed | Portfolio management â€” close/fix/transfer them |
| Inconsistent quality | Food OS + Tech OS â€” standardize operations |
| Weak operators visible | Tiering system â€” Tier 3 exits, Tier 1 rewarded |
| Data integrity questions | Data Governance â€” trustworthy metrics |
| "What if we look bad?" | Right-size to 12-16k elite stores first |

**The strategy exists to make transparency survivable â€” and powerful.**

## Why This Could Only Work at Subway

**Scale creates the moat.**

| Factor | Subway's Position | Competitor Reality |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Store count** | 20,000+ US locations | Most chains have 2,000-8,000 |
| **Franchise model** | 99% franchised | Many are corporate-heavy |

| **Perception problem** | Already seen as inconsistent | Transparency is a recovery play, not a risk |
| **Data infrastructure** | Building unified Tech OS | Most lack real-time operational data |

**The counterintuitive advantage:**

Subway's biggest weakness (inconsistency across 20k stores) becomes its biggest differentiator. By exposing performance publicly:

1. **Good stores shine** â€” Top performers finally get recognized
2. **Bad stores face pressure** â€” Customer visibility forces improvement or exit
3. **Customers become partners** â€” They help identify what's working
4. **Competitors can't follow** â€” They'd expose their own inconsistencies

*"If you're going to be transparent, you better be good. And if you're going to be good, you might as well be transparent."*

## Franchisee-Franchisor: From Landlord to Partner

**The current reality:**

| Who | Bears | Controls |
|-----|-------|----------|
| **Franchisor (Corporate)** | Brand risk | Menu, marketing, pricing, standards, site approval |
| **Franchisee (Operator)** | All financial risk | Almost nothing |

Franchisees carry the debt. Franchisees sign the leases. Franchisees pay the labor. Franchisees deal with angry customers when a $5 footlong promotion tanks their margins.

Yet they have minimal say in the decisions that determine whether they succeed or fail.

**This is not partnership. This is tenancy.**

### The Imbalance

| Decision | Who Decides | Who Pays |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| National promotions | Corporate | Franchisee margins |
| Menu changes | Corporate | Franchisee labor/training |
| Remodel mandates | Corporate | Franchisee capex |
| Technology requirements | Corporate | Franchisee implementation |
| Pricing strategies | Corporate | Franchisee P&L |

*"We make the rules. You take the risk."*

### The Partnership Model

**For the Subway Comeback to work, both parties must have skin in the game â€” and voice in decisions.**

| Element | Old Model | Partnership Model |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Decision-making** | Top-down mandates | Franchisee councils with real authority |
| **Risk sharing** | Franchisee bears all | Shared investment in remodels, closures |
| **Transparency** | Corporate sees franchisee P&L | Franchisees see corporate economics too |
| **Promotions** | "Run this or else" | "Here's the data â€” let's decide together" |
| **Innovation** | "We tested it, you run it" | "Let's test it together in your store" |
| **Closures** | "Your problem" | "Our shared portfolio decision" |

### What Partnership Looks Like (from Subway-v2)

**Already in the strategic framework:**

1. **Franchisee Advisory Council** â€” Bounded authority over menu, marketing, ops (not just "advisory")

2. **Consolidation & Reinvestment Fund** â€” Corporate co-invests in:
   - Remodel compliance (royalty relief, co-funding)
   - Exit packages (up to $50k for managed closures)
   - Relocations (capex co-funds up to $250k)

3. **Tiering with Support** â€” Tier 2 stores get help, not just pressure:
   - Clear improvement roadmap
   - Time-bound milestones
   - Resources to succeed

4. **Transparent Benchmarking** â€” Franchisees see:
   - How their store compares regionally/nationally
   - What top performers do differently
   - Economic impact of decisions before they're made

5. **Test â†’ Prove â†’ Scale** â€” Franchisees participate in testing:
   - Tier 1 operators pilot new initiatives
   - Real data before system-wide rollout
   - Kill criteria defined upfront (not "we're doing this regardless")

### The New Social Contract

**Franchisor commits to:**
- No margin-killing promotions without franchisee input
- Co-investment in major capex requirements
- Transparent economics (show your math)
- Real voice in decisions that affect P&L
- Support for struggling stores, not just pressure

**Franchisee commits to:**
- Meet operational standards
- Invest in their store (image, tech, training)
- Participate in system initiatives
- Share performance data
- Embrace accountability (tiering, transparency)

**Both commit to:**
- The comeback narrative
- Customer partnership (transparency, participation)
- Portfolio over individual interests
- Long-term brand health over short-term gains

*"If franchisees carry the debt, they deserve a seat at the table where decisions about that debt are made."*
